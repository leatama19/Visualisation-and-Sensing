In the Philippines, the word bagyo means typhoon. It refers to tropical cyclones that form in the Pacific Ocean and regularly affect the country, often bringing destructive winds, heavy rainfall, and widespread flooding. Typhoons are a recurring and significant natural hazard in the region. Each year, multiple storms impact communities across the Philippines, with varying levels of intensity but often severe consequences for infrastructure, agriculture, and human life. As climate patterns shift, it becomes increasingly important to understand whether these storms are changing in strength, frequency, or impact. However, looking at individual events alone makes it difficult to see the broader patterns over time.
